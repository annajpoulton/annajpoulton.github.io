About Me

I am a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at Cornell University, advised by Dr. Stephen Ellner. My research is focused on developing and utilizing mathematical models to inform ecological management and conservation. I use methods from dynamical systems, optimization, and statistics in my work to study ecological systems.

In a recently published work, we used a multi-patch fisheries model to investigate the performance of dynamic spatial closures under fishing-mediated habitat damage. We found that dynamic closures can achieve greater harvests and more habitat protection than fixed closures in many scenarios, especially when fish respond strongly to habitat quality.

Currently I am working on projects focused on (1) Bayesian spatial survival analysis, and (2) the role of behavior in host-pathogen dynamics.
